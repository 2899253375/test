Introduction

Considering the current limitations of style datasets in general object detection, which are modest in size and have limited categories, we introduce MSOSB: a Multi-Style Object Search Benchmark. MSOSB aligns with MS COCO in terms of category setup but includes a diverse array of styles: cartoon, oil, water-color, pencil, and art.

The images for MSOSB is sourced from a wide range of origins: it comprises 999 images from Clipart, 1905 from Watercolor, 1905 from Comic, 264 from PACS, 170 from OfficeHome, 1004 from the internet, 37624 generated by stable diffusion, and 32275 transformed by Baidu's style conversion API, totaling 75146 images. This comprehensive and stylistically diverse dataset is designed to rigorously test and enhance the domain generalization capabilities of current object detection models.

Characteristics

The MSOSB introduces a series of challenging settings that rigorously test the limits of CDOS models, with challenges arising from:

Variability in object size and aspect ratio. The MSOSB encompasses objects of varying sizes, from minuscule to those occupying major portions of the image, along with a diverse range of aspect ratios. This variability demands that models accurately search for objects that might span vast areas or just a small part of the visual space, challenging their spatial recognition capabilities.

Complex backgrounds and object overlap. Images within the MSOSB typically feature complex backgrounds and overlapping objects, mirroring real-world scenarios where objects of interest do not exist in isolation but are embedded within dense and visually noisy environments. This tests the models' ability to effectively distinguish and isolate targets from their surrounding context.

Multi-category and multi-object scenes. Each image in the MSOSB may contain multiple objects across different categories, requiring models to have robust object classification and localization capabilities.

Cross-Domain Variability. MSOSB shares categories with MS COCO, allowing it to be used in conjunction with MS COCO to further challenge the domain generalization capabilities of CDOS models. This includes adapting to various visual styles as well as to changes in object contexts and environmental factors, reflecting the unpredictable variations encountered in real-world applications.

These challenging configurations are carefully designed to simulate complex visual scenarios, thereby providing a comprehensive benchmark for advancing cross-domain object detection technologies in the fields of deep learning and computer vision.
